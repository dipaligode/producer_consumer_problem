Topic: Producer-Consumer Problem

The Producer-Consumer Problem is a classic example of synchronization in multi-processing, ensuring that the producer and consumer processes use a common buffer efficiently without conflicts or data corruption.

💡 Theory: Producer-Consumer Problem (Short Points)

🔁 Problem: Producer and Consumer share a fixed-size buffer. Need synchronization to avoid conflict.
📦 Shared Buffer: Temporary storage where producer adds and consumer removes items.
🧑‍🔧 Producer: Produces items and adds to buffer. Waits if buffer is full.
🧑‍🏭 Consumer: Consumes items from buffer. Waits if buffer is empty.
🔒 Synchronization: Ensures buffer is accessed safely by one process at a time.
🚫 Buffer Full: Producer stops and waits. Resumes when space is available.
🈳 Buffer Empty: Consumer stops and waits. Resumes when new item is produced.
🛠 Tools Used: Java, JSP, HTML, Tailwind CSS, Semaphores, Threading.
